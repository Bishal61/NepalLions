Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about your Himalayan adventure

We recommend booking at least 2-3 months in advance for peak seasons (March-May & September-November). For custom treks and peak climbing, 4-6 months is ideal to ensure proper permits and guide availability.

All packages include: professional licensed guide, all accommodations (3-star hotels & teahouses during trek), all meals during trek, necessary permits, airport transfers, and ground transportation. International flights and travel insurance are not included unless specified.

Cancel 30+ days before: 90% refund. Cancel 15-29 days: 50% refund. Cancel less than 15 days: no refund. Refunds are processed within 14 business days. We strongly recommend travel insurance for unexpected changes.

Nepal: Visa on arrival available (USD 30 for 15 days, USD 50 for 30 days). Bhutan: Mandatory daily sustainable tariff (USD 100-250/day) required. We assist with all visa arrangements.

Fitness requirements vary: Easy (Poon Hill) needs basic fitness. Moderate (Annapurna Circuit) needs regular cardio. Difficult (Everest Base Camp) needs excellent fitness & altitude acclimatization. We rate each trip and provide preparation guidelines.

Absolutely! All our packages are customizable. Contact us with your preferences - we can adjust duration, difficulty, accommodation type, add/remove destinations, and tailor every detail to match your vision.

All guides are first-aid trained with wilderness certification. We carry emergency oxygen, SAT phone, and comprehensive first-aid kits. Routes are monitored daily, and we have 24/7 office support. All treks include altitude acclimatization schedules.

Spring (Mar-May): Perfect weather, rhododendron bloom. Autumn (Sep-Nov): Clear skies, best mountain views. Summer/Monsoon: Lush green, fewer crowds, landslides possible. Winter: Snow at altitude, great for lower treks.
